Stretching & recovery basics

A little bit of warm-up and mobility goes a long way. These are simple, general ideas—skip any movement that causes sharp pain.

Before your workout – warm-up

  • 3–5 minutes of easy movement (walk, cycle, jog).
  • Dynamic moves: arm circles, leg swings, hip circles.
  • 1–2 light sets of your first exercise as a “rehearsal”.

After your workout – cool-down

  • Gentle walking and deep breathing.
  • Static stretching: hold each stretch for ~20–30 seconds.
  • Focus on muscles you trained: chest, back, legs, hips.

Desk & daily life mobility

  • Every hour, stand up, roll your shoulders, and walk a bit.
  • Hip flexor stretch if you sit a lot.
  • Cat-cow and gentle twists for your upper back.
  • Ankle circles and calf stretches for better walking and squats.

If you’re injured or in pain, see a medical professional before exercising.
