Resource library & references

These links are examples of the kinds of high-quality resources that inspired the information on this site. Always check with a qualified professional for personal advice.

1. Overall physical activity guidelines

  • CDC – Physical Activity Guidelines for Adults – Official recommendations for weekly cardio and strength training.
  • CDC – Adding Physical Activity to Your Life – Practical ideas for fitting movement into a busy schedule.
  • WHO – Physical Activity Fact Sheet – Global overview of why being active matters for health.

2. Workout planning & exercise selection

  • NHS – How to Improve Strength and Flexibility – Examples of strength exercises and how often to do them.
  • NHS – Strength and Flex Exercise Plan (with videos) – Follow-along routines you can adapt for home workouts.
  • ACE Exercise Library – Searchable database of exercises with form cues and filters.

3. Strength training, muscle & progressive overload

  • Examine – Evidence-based guide to muscle growth – How volume, intensity, and recovery affect hypertrophy.
  • Stronger By Science – Hypertrophy rep ranges – Explains why many rep ranges can work if training is hard enough.

4. Nutrition basics & healthy plates

  • Harvard T.H. Chan – Healthy Eating Plate – Visual guide for building balanced meals.
  • Harvard Health – How to use the Healthy Eating Plate – More detail on each part of the plate.
  • Examine – Dietary Protein Guide – Evidence-based overview of protein needs and sources.

5. Eating for fat loss & body recomposition

  • Examine – How to Lose Fat (guide) – Explains energy balance and sustainable calorie deficits.
  • EatingWell – Habit to Build Muscle (protein focus) – Practical ideas for getting enough protein across the day.

6. Stretching, mobility & recovery

  • Current Concepts in Muscle Stretching – Review article discussing different stretching methods.
  • Stretching Before and After Exercise – Effect on Injury Risk – What stretching can and can’t do for injury prevention.
  • NHS – Strength and Flexibility Videos – Good demos of safe stretching and mobility work.

7. Mindset, motivation & behavior change

  • CDC – Physical Activity Basics and Your Health – Simple explanation of how moving more improves mood and energy.
  • James Clear – Atomic Habits summary – How tiny consistent habits beat motivation bursts.

8. Safety, medical considerations & general health

  • CDC – Getting Started with Physical Activity – When to talk to a doctor, pacing yourself, and staying safe.
  • NHS – Exercise Health Benefits – Overview of how regular activity affects long-term health.

Using and updating this page

As you keep learning, you can add your own favorite sources here: research papers, trusted coaches, or videos that match your approach. Think of this page as the “behind the scenes” reference list for your whole site.
